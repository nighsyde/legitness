## Appendix C — Family Protocols (Copy and Adapt)

### Protocol 1 — Pay-Yourself-First Automation
1. On payday, fixed percentages route to: reserves, investments, giving, taxable spending account.  
2. Lifestyle spends only from spending account.  
3. Raises split: ≥50% to future, remainder to present.  

### Protocol 2 — Large Purchase Rule
Any purchase above **X** (set your number) requires:
- 30-day wait (except true emergencies)  
- Written purpose  
- Opportunity-cost note (“this equals ___ months of investing”)  
- Partner assent if household-shared  

### Protocol 3 — Windfall Rule
For bonus, inheritance, liquidity event, legal settlement:
1. 30–90 day cooling period  
2. Park in safe instruments  
3. Allocate by preset policy (example: 40% invest, 20% reserves/debt, 20% long-term goals, 10% give, 10% enjoy)  
4. No public boasting

## Appendix E — The Anti-Patterns (Memorize the Traps)

1. **Income masquerading as wealth** — high salary, no surplus  
2. **Leverage masquerading as genius** — borrowed upside, personal downside  
3. **Busyness masquerading as progress** — motion without capital formation  
4. **Complexity masquerading as sophistication** — products you can’t explain  
5. **Optimism masquerading as a plan** — no margin of safety  
6. **Frugality masquerading as fear** — refusing all investment in self/assets  
7. **Generosity masquerading as chaos** — giving without solvency  
8. **Love masquerading as secrecy** — hiding money until death creates war  
9. **Confidence masquerading as competence** — concentrated bets without edge  
10. **Legacy masquerading as control** — ruling heirs instead of preparing them  

When you feel smart and rushed, stop. Smart + rushed is how fortunes evaporate.

## Appendix F — A 90-Day Ignition Plan

### Days 1–7: Truth
- List all accounts, debts, income, expenses  
- Calculate net worth  
- Read front matter + Seven Laws daily  

### Days 8–30: Stability
- Build or top up emergency reserves automation  
- Kill or plan destruction of high-interest debt  
- Write IPS draft  
- Start or raise automated investing to a non-negotiable floor  

### Days 31–60: Earnings & Skills
- Identify one high-value skill gap  
- Schedule weekly deep practice  
- Negotiate or plan one income-improving move  
- Cut three envy expenses; redirect to surplus  

### Days 61–90: Ownership & Teaching
- Open/optimize long-term investment accounts as appropriate  
- Buy (or deepen) ownership: index core, business learning, or property education—not random tips  
- Teach one chapter to someone younger  
- Hold first family money meeting  
- Schedule annual risk review on calendar  

At day 90, do not celebrate by buying a costume. Celebrate by writing what you will still be doing in year 10.

## Appendix G — Sample Letters to Descendants

### Letter at Age 16
You are old enough to know that money is a tool and a test. Save part of every earning. Learn a skill people need. Do not mock quiet wealth. Read Clason this month. Come back with questions.

### Letter at First Job
Automate surplus before you decorate your life. Contribute enough to capture any employer match. Build reserves. Your coworkers’ lifestyles are not research. Your future household is.

### Letter at Marriage / Partnership
Merge missions before merging extravagance. Agree on debt, giving, and freedom numbers. Protect the relationship from silent scorekeeping. Wealth is a third party in your home—make it a servant, not a rival.

### Letter at Windfall
Do not become a different person in public. Become a more careful person in private. Wait. Allocate by policy. Call counsel. Keep your friends by keeping your humility.

### Letter at Inheritance Receipt
This money contains someone’s lifetime. Honor it with craft, not display. Read the ethical will before the brokerage statement.

## Appendix H — Glossary for Heirs

**Asset** — Something that materially improves future capability or cash flow.  
**Liability** — A claim that obligates future cash or attention.  
**Compounding** — Earnings on prior earnings over time.  
**Margin of safety** — Room for being wrong without ruin.  
**Optionality** — Ability to choose later without being forced now.  
**Ruin risk** — Chance of irreversible failure.  
**Surplus** — Income above true expenses.  
**Stewardship** — Managing resources as a trust for purposes beyond ego.  
**Enough** — The point of independence where more is chosen, not chased.  
**Skin in the game** — Bearing consequences of one’s advice or bets.
