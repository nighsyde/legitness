# PART XXVI — DETAILED METHODS LIBRARY

*Tactics beneath principles. Adjust instruments to your century; keep the method.*

---

## Method 1 — The Surplus Ladder

Raise savings rate in rungs, not leaps that snap.

Example ladder: 10% → 15% → 20% → 25% → 30%+ in high seasons.

Trigger rung climbs at:

- Each raise (capture half)  
- Each debt payoff (redirect payment to surplus)  
- Each completed lifestyle experiment that failed the 30-day test  

Never climb a rung by creating credit card float. That is cosplay discipline.

---

## Method 2 — The Two-Account Life

**Operating account:** bills and planned spending.  
**Freedom account(s):** reserves and investments.

Income lands; automation splits; you live on what remains. This single design has created more quiet millionaires than stock tips.

Bach’s automaticity + Clear’s environment design = fewer heroic mornings.

---

## Method 3 — The Career Sprint / Recover Cycle

Work in seasons:

- Sprint: skill intensity, networking, shipping proof  
- Recover: health, relationships, reading, consolidation  

Permanent sprint becomes medical debt and divorce risk—both financial events. Permanent recover becomes stagnation. Cycle deliberately.

---

## Method 4 — The Opportunity Filter

Score opportunities 1–5 on:

- Expected value  
- Ruin risk (invert score)  
- Time tax  
- Ethical clarity  
- Learning value  
- Fit with IPS / mission  

Proceed only if weighted total clears your pre-set bar. If you cannot score it, you cannot size it.

---

## Method 5 — The Concentration Governor

Hard cap any single non-core position at X% of investable assets (choose X you can survive).  
Hard cap employer stock if applicable.  
Hard cap private deals as a sleeve.

Governors exist because future-you will argue with present-you’s prudence.

---

## Method 6 — The Fee Hunt

Annually list every recurring fee:

- Fund expenses  
- Advisors  
- Bank fees  
- Subscriptions  
- Insurance priced wrong  

Kill or renegotiate. Bogle’s ghost applauds dull hunts.

---

## Method 7 — The Knowledge CapEx Budget

Allocate a % of income to learning and tools that raise earning power: courses, books, equipment, coaches with skin in outcomes.

This is not permission for endless seminar addiction. Require application within 30 days of purchase.

---

## Method 8 — The Generosity Percentage Plus Projects

Base: fixed % of surplus to giving.  
Plus: one project yearly you can see—scholarship, local need, mentorship hours.

Measurement prevents both coldness and chaotic savior syndrome.

---

## Method 9 — The Family Bank (Advanced; Use Caution)

Some families create an internal bank for education and enterprise loans with:

- Written applications  
- Interest or matching rules  
- Reporting  
- Default policies that do not destroy Thanksgiving  

If governance is weak, do not build a family bank; build clearer gifts and clearer nos.

---

## Method 10 — The Annual “Kill List”

Once a year, kill:

- One expense that doesn’t serve mission  
- One half-dead project  
- One toxic obligation you can ethically exit  
- One vanity metric  

Pruning is a wealth skill.

---

## Method 11 — The Pre-Mortem

Before major moves, write:

> Assume it is three years later and this failed. Why?

List causes. Mitigate what you can. Proceed only if residual risk is survivable.

Munger’s inversion in paperwork form.

---

## Method 12 — The Second Opinion Rule

For decisions above threshold, require one skeptical second opinion from a competent person who does not earn a commission if you say yes.

If you cannot find such a person, you are already isolated—fix that first.

# PART XXVIII — FINAL SYNTHESIS: THE CLOSED LOOP OF WEALTH

Wealth that lasts runs a loop:

**Character → Usefulness → Income → Surplus → Ownership → Time → Independence → Stewardship → Teaching → Character in the next generation**

Break the loop at any arrow and the dynasty becomes a rumor.

- Character without usefulness becomes private virtue with public dependency.  
- Income without surplus becomes a well-paid treadmill.  
- Surplus without ownership becomes a mattress.  
- Ownership without time becomes gambling.  
- Independence without stewardship becomes soft decay.  
- Stewardship without teaching becomes a one-generation miracle.  

Close the loop. Then close it again.

That is the whole book in one breath.
