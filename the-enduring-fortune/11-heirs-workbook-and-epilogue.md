# EPILOGUE — THE HOUSE WITH A LONG TABLE

I imagine a house far from now—your house—with a long table.

Someone young complains that wealth talk is uncomfortable. Someone older opens this book anyway. They read a law aloud. Someone laughs at my old-fashioned sentences. Someone else goes quiet because a line cuts close.

That quiet is the point.

I cannot protect you from history. I can give you a craft that has protected many families from the smaller tragedies: avoidable poverty, avoidable fragility, avoidable greed, avoidable silence.

Build the house.  
Set the long table.  
Feed people.  
Teach them.

And when your turn comes to be the elder, do not merely leave accounts.

Leave a way.

**— Grandfather**
