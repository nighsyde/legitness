# PART XX — STRATEGY CARDS BY LIFE STAGE

*Keep these short. Use them like a pilot’s checklist.*

---

## Age ~16–22 — Foundation Cards

**F1 Earn** — Get real responsibility for money through work.  
**F2 Split** — Every inflow: save / give / spend with written %.  
**F3 Learn** — Clason + Franklin + one skill book in your intended field.  
**F4 Avoid** — Status debt, cosigning, “easy” money schemes.  
**F5 Build** — Reputation for reliability; it will pay for decades.  

## Age ~23–35 — Acceleration Cards

**A1 Automate** — Raise savings rate before lifestyle rate.  
**A2 Capture match** — Take free compensation matches where they exist.  
**A3 Negotiate** — Job changes and raises with research and calm.  
**A4 Own** — Begin lifetime diversified ownership habit.  
**A5 Experiment** — Small ownership experiments (side enterprise, IP) sized to survive failure.  
**A6 Partner** — Money constitution if sharing a life.  

## Age ~36–50 — Architecture Cards

**R1 Systems** — Document money OS; reduce key-person fragility at home.  
**R2 Edge** — Only concentrate where competence is real.  
**R3 Tax craft** — Legal optimization with clean books; no clever nonsense.  
**R4 Teach** — Children/ junior relatives enter apprenticeship.  
**R5 Insure** — Revisit ruin insurance as income and dependents grow.  
**R6 Enough draft** — First serious independence number.  

## Age ~51–65 — Fortification Cards

**T1 Simplify** — Complexity must earn its keep.  
**T2 Estate** — Documents, beneficiaries, ethical will.  
**T3 Governance** — Family meeting becomes real, not symbolic.  
**T4 Health** — Treat body as non-diversifiable asset.  
**T5 Mentorship** — Transfer judgment, not only assets.  
**T6 Recalibrate** — Work intensity by choice, not inertia.  

## Age ~66+ — Stewardship Cards

**S1 Witness** — Tell the stories behind the numbers.  
**S2 Staged transfer** — Observe heirs with real responsibility while alive.  
**S3 Narrow bets** — Reduce ego concentration.  
**S4 Bless** — Give appreciation and clear expectations.  
**S5 Archive** — Update this book’s addendum pages.  
**S6 Enjoy** — Spend planned joy without apology.  

---

## Universal Cards (Any Age)

**U1** — When confused, increase runway and decrease fixed costs.  
**U2** — When euphoric, reduce new risk.  
**U3** — When despairing, obey written rules, not feelings.  
**U4** — When ashamed, seek truth and repair, not display.  
**U5** — When proud, seek a critic who loves you.  
**U6** — When lonely at the top, you built a tower instead of a table—start inviting and serving.

# PART XXI — CASE STUDIES (COMPOSITES)

*Not saints. Patterns.*

---

## Case Study A — The High Earner With Nothing

A surgeon earned like aristocracy and lived like a storefront of success. No reserves, floating-rate toys, no plan. A hand injury and a divorce arrived in the same era. Income fell; fixed costs did not.

**What would have changed everything:** surplus automation, smaller house ratio, disability insurance, money constitution, identity not fused to display.

**Law violated:** 1, 5, 6.

---

## Case Study B — The Quiet Couple

Teachers with ordinary incomes, extreme consistency, low-cost ownership over decades, home they could afford, children taught early. They never trended. They became free in their fifties.

**What mattered:** savings rate, time, refusal to interrupt, boredom tolerance.

**Law honored:** 1–4, 7.

---

## Case Study C — The Entrepreneur Who Won Then Lost

Built a beloved company, sold, felt invincible, concentrated the fortune into a fashionable speculative pile with leverage. Did not keep a permanent Independence Core. The second fortune vanished; the first had been real.

**What would have changed everything:** bucket policy, windfall protocol, humility tax (mandatory diversification of life-changing liquidity).

**Law violated:** 4, 5.

---

## Case Study D — The Heir Who Made It Real

Inherited a sum that could have funded a soft life. Instead apprenticed in operations, kept living costs modest for five years, invested the corpus under IPS, built a small firm, taught siblings. Friends called them boring. Their grandchildren will call them founders of a second stage.

**What mattered:** apprenticeship before consumption, governance, teaching.

**Law honored:** 6, 7 especially.

---

## Case Study E — The Fraud That Looked Like Community

A circle of friends entered a “private income club” with smooth returns and recruitment rewards. Social trust replaced verification. Custody was opaque. It ended as these things end.

**Defense that was available:** independent custody, skepticism of smoothness, refusal to recruit under pressure, reading Taleb and basic due diligence aloud like a spell against belonging-need.

**Law violated:** 5, 6.

---

## Case Study F — The Family That Fought the Will

A parent died with ambiguity, secret unequal gifts, and no ethical will. Children litigated for years. Lawyers bought second homes. The family’s name became a caution in town.

**Defense:** clarity while alive, fairness explained, staged conversations, documents, letters.

**Law violated:** 7.
