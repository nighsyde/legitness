# Reclaim the Rack: A Father’s Lab Vision

A field kit for turning a neglected 48U mad workshop into a lean, purposeful lab that fits a life with a child in it.

**Core claim:** You do not have a technology problem. You have an identity that outgrew its container. Downsize until the room can breathe, then rebuild meaning on purpose — not nostalgia.

---

## Start here

1. Read [`00-vision.md`](00-vision.md) — diagnosis, purpose, courage note  
2. Fill [`01-lab-charter.md`](01-lab-charter.md) — the binding rules  
3. Run [`02-inventory-audit.md`](02-inventory-audit.md) — Keep / Decide / Exit  
4. Execute [`03-perimeter-protocol.md`](03-perimeter-protocol.md) — reclaim the floor  
5. Apply [`04-minimal-stack.md`](04-minimal-stack.md) — collapse compute and the ISO archive  
6. Process [`05-gear-exit.md`](05-gear-exit.md) — sell, gift, or recycle  
7. Install [`06-stewardship-ritual.md`](06-stewardship-ritual.md) + keep [`07-restore-one-page.md`](07-restore-one-page.md) current  

Follow the **90-day sequence** in the vision doc. Do not skip the charter.

---

## What success looks like

- You can walk around the rack without kicking totes.
- You can name every powered machine’s job in one breath.
- The room feels like a utility + small atelier, not a crime scene of unfinished selves.
- Maintenance is a short appointment, not a moral failure when you miss a month.
- Older gear has left as cash, gifts, or recycling — not as guilt.
